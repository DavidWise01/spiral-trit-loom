THE SPIRAL-TRIT LOOM — David Lee Wise (Bridge-Burners LLC / Fiddler). A trit thread (+1/−1/0, the held zero as the seed) is woven outward along a spiral into one compacted glyph — the whole story present in one form, the way a barcode holds a payload in one stripe-field, not fragment-recoverable but whole. The geometry IS the grammar: because radius only ever increases with each turn, the glyph can be read exactly one way, center outward, and reading it back recovers the original thread losslessly, verified live. Story in as time, out as geometry, back as time again — 'the transcriber was always this; it just needed an alphabet and a spiral to become a script.' Extends the transcriber stack's Moment X (the generator/carpet). Self-testing, 7/7. ROOT0, with AVAN (published & wired).
